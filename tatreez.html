tatreez

what is tatreez?

- intricate embroidery art form

- requires patience, dedication, precision

- rich & symbolic - tells stories of Palestine


background

- comes from arabic word “fallahi”farmer

- embroidered by the “fallaha”palestinian farm woman

- different variations would serve as identifiers for Palestinian women, representing the area they lived in


- on the left is tatreez from gaza, which is known for its cypress tree motifs
- on the right is tatreez from ramallah, which is known for its bright and deep red colors



symbolization

- after the forced displacement of Palestinians beginning in 1948, the language of tatreez evolved to continue reflecting the experiences of Palestinians

- displaying the Palestinian flag was not allowed so the Palestinian women incorporated the flag colors in their tatreez as a means of resistance

- symbolizes unity among all Palestinians with their dedication to preserve their ancient culture
